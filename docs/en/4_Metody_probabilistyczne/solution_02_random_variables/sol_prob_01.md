# Task 1

Let $X$ denote the grade (2–5) of a randomly selected student.

Given grades:

$$
\{5,4,3,3,4,5,3,3,4,2\}
$$

## 1. Is $X$ a random variable?

Yes.  
A random variable assigns a numerical value to each outcome of a random experiment.

Experiment: randomly selecting one student.  
Value assigned: the student's grade.

Therefore,

$$
X = \text{grade of a randomly selected student}
$$

---

## 2. Possible values

From the data:

$$
X \in \{2,3,4,5\}
$$

---

## 3. Probability distribution

Count each grade:

| Grade | Count |
|------|------|
|2|1|
|3|4|
|4|3|
|5|2|

Total students: $10$

$$
P(X=x) = \frac{\text{count}}{10}
$$

| $x$ | 2 | 3 | 4 | 5 |
|----|----|----|----|----|
| $P(X=x)$ | 0.1 | 0.4 | 0.3 | 0.2 |

Check:

$$
0.1 + 0.4 + 0.3 + 0.2 = 1
$$

The probabilities sum to 1, so the distribution is correct.
