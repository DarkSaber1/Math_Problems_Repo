# Task 8

Given:

For person 1:

$$
P(X=1)=0.6, \qquad P(X=2)=0.4
$$

For person 2:

$$
P(Y=1)=0.5, \qquad P(Y=2)=0.5
$$

The variables \(X\) and \(Y\) are independent.

---

# 1. Use independence

For independent variables:

$$
P(X=x, Y=y)=P(X=x)\cdot P(Y=y)
$$

Now calculate each joint probability.

$$
P(X=1,Y=1)=0.6\cdot 0.5=0.3
$$

$$
P(X=1,Y=2)=0.6\cdot 0.5=0.3
$$

$$
P(X=2,Y=1)=0.4\cdot 0.5=0.2
$$

$$
P(X=2,Y=2)=0.4\cdot 0.5=0.2
$$

---

# 2. Joint distribution table

| \(Y \backslash X\) | 1 | 2 |
|---|---:|---:|
| **1** | 0.3 | 0.2 |
| **2** | 0.3 | 0.2 |

Check:

$$
0.3+0.3+0.2+0.2=1
$$

---

# Final Answer

The joint distribution of \((X,Y)\) is:

| \(Y \backslash X\) | 1 | 2 |
|---|---:|---:|
| **1** | 0.3 | 0.2 |
| **2** | 0.3 | 0.2 |

Equivalently,

$$
P(X=1,Y=1)=0.3
$$

$$
P(X=1,Y=2)=0.3
$$

$$
P(X=2,Y=1)=0.2
$$

$$
P(X=2,Y=2)=0.2
$$
