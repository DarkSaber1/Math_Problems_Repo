## Solution

Three plants produce a certain product.  
The probabilities that a product from each plant is of first quality are:

- Plant 1: $p_1 = 0.97$
- Plant 2: $p_2 = 0.90$
- Plant 3: $p_3 = 0.86$

One product from each plant is taken, so we have three products in total.  
A randomly selected item is chosen from these three.

Since each product has the same probability $\frac{1}{3}$ of being selected, we apply the law of total probability.

\[
P(F) = \frac{1}{3} \cdot 0.97 + \frac{1}{3} \cdot 0.90 + \frac{1}{3} \cdot 0.86
\]

\[
P(F) = \frac{0.97 + 0.90 + 0.86}{3}
\]

\[
P(F) = \frac{2.73}{3}
\]

\[
P(F) = 0.91
\]

### Final answer

The probability that the randomly selected item is of first quality is:

$P(F) = 0.91$
