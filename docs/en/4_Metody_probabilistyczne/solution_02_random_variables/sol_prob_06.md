# Task 6

The two-dimensional random variable \((X,Y)\) is defined as:

- \(X=1\) if the large gear is **plus**, \(X=0\) if **minus**
- \(Y=1\) if the small gear is **plus**, \(Y=0\) if **minus**

Given probabilities:

$$
P(X=0,Y=0)=\frac{1}{4}
$$

$$
P(X=1,Y=1)=\frac{1}{4}
$$

$$
P(X=0,Y=1)=\frac{1}{4}
$$

$$
P(X=1,Y=0)=\frac{1}{4}
$$

---

# 1. Joint distribution table

| \(Y \backslash X\) | 0 | 1 |
|---|---:|---:|
| **0** | \(\frac{1}{4}\) | \(\frac{1}{4}\) |
| **1** | \(\frac{1}{4}\) | \(\frac{1}{4}\) |

---

# 2. Probability that the assembly is correct

The assembly is **correct** when one gear is plus and the other is minus:

- \((X=0,Y=1)\)
- \((X=1,Y=0)\)

So,

$$
P(\text{correct assembly}) = P(X=0,Y=1) + P(X=1,Y=0)
$$

$$
P(\text{correct assembly}) = \frac{1}{4} + \frac{1}{4}
$$

$$
P(\text{correct assembly}) = \frac{1}{2}
$$

---

# Final Answer

## Joint distribution

| \(Y \backslash X\) | 0 | 1 |
|---|---:|---:|
| **0** | \(\frac{1}{4}\) | \(\frac{1}{4}\) |
| **1** | \(\frac{1}{4}\) | \(\frac{1}{4}\) |

## Probability of correct assembly

$$
P(\text{correct assembly})=\frac{1}{2}
$$
